## Interpretation

This materially narrows the previous ambiguity:

- `vi` is not absent for the whole timeout window;
- Jetson-side clock bookkeeping shows both `NVCSI` and `VI` clocks active
  during the failed capture attempt;
- yet receiver-side ingress tracepoints remain completely silent.

So the new evidence is **not**:

- "VI never turned on"

The new evidence is:

- Jetson-side receiver clocks come up,
- but no valid frame ingress becomes visible to the tracepoints.

## Practical Conclusion

This strengthens the existing root-cause boundary:

- the failure is later than simple power-domain non-entry;
- the failure is earlier than any visible `SOF/EOF` or `vi_frame_begin`;
- the project is now deep into diminishing returns for Jetson-side software-only
  introspection on the current hardware path.
